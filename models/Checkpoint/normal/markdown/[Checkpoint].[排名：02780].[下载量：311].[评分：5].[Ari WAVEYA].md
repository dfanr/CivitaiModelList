Face is able to look around better, and model freaks out less when in outdoor environments.</p><p>If grainy or blurry, use the negative prompts "low resolution, jpeg artifacts, motion blur".</p><p>If the skin looks really plastic like, using the negative prompt "unreal engine, cg" will help out.<br />Still has issues with being overexposed, since all their videos (where I get the frames to train) have really strong lighting.</p><p></p><p><strong>Training method:</strong></p><p>Model was trained over several different steps. First with close ups for "face, facial expression", then fullbody photos for "woman, body", and then a different set for "woman". There were a few more steps, but that's the basic idea.</p><p>A ton of images were used, around 700'ish individual ones, hence the huge step count.</p><p>The base model is a custom merge.</p>
