<h3>G8 OpenPoseRig, Final version, (barring bug fixes), V1.0</h3><p>Bunch of QOL fixes.</p><p>Eliminated the 'prop bones' and managed keep the torso from exploding without them, should make it more suitable for exporting. Very weird poses can cause the torso bones to stick out at the neck but who bends both forward and backwards at the same time eh?</p><p>Some quality of life refinements on the icons. Improved the skeleton thickness options. More shaping morphs, most proprtions can be adjusted in some way. If theres something you can't get it to do try scaling the bones individually.</p><p> It will fit to G8F without complaint now. If you add extreme morphs the skeleton mesh will distort due to the morph projection not being able to get a 1:1 correlation but it will be usable for most things, even if it is a bit lumpy. Honestly you're better off matching the proportions using the sliders without fitting it, then saving that as a pose/morph preset.</p><p>Anyway, here it is, as the French say.</p><p>.................................</p><p></p><p>I suck at tutorials, so if anyone can write up a good work flow please do so. Render Properties &gt; Color Management &gt; Standard</p><p>Turn on node wrangler in addons as shown in above video and add the viewer node.</p><p>Add an image node to compositor and select your .exr the background of the compositor.</p><p>Go to the top rendering tab and save out as the default png file.</p><p>Open it in gimp or similar paint program and invert it and overwrite save.</p><p>Use depth map in controlnet with 'none' selected under preprocessor and the depth model along with the pose image from daz and a openpose model in the second controlnet.</p><p><a target="_blank" rel="ugc" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MDHC7E6G1RA">Watch video on using multi controlnet if need be.</a></p><p><strong>Note:</strong> I tried doing it all in either gimp or blenders compositor but both had issues getting a properly normalized and inverted png out.</p><p>Maybe someone else can come up with a easier solution?</p><p>Hopefully this will be helpful to at least get going and maybe
